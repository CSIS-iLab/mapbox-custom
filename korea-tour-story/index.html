Haebang Market

A 50-year old man working as a laborer at a construction company. He reports more than 75 percent of his household income is derived from the jangmadang. Forced labor mobilization, so-called "non-tax payments," and insufficient wages causes him to feel great animosity toward the North Korean regime.

Nagyang Market

A 45-year old man working as a printing factory worker. He reports almost all of his household income is derived from the jangmadang. He values freedom and equality and says the suppression of human rights causes him to feel the greatest animosity toward the North Korean regime.

Munhwa Market

A 48-year old women working in pubic at the Chongjin Women's and Children's Factory. She reports almost all of her household income is derived from the jangmadang. “The whole society is built to sustain the cadres. The three generations of Kim rule has wrecked havoc on the lives of ordinary people,” she says.

Namyang-Tomun Rail Bridge

A 48-year old gentlemen working in the trade sector. He earned a high school degree. When asked which would he would rather have: More nuclear weapons or more goods in the markets, he replied more goods. He looks to the United States as the country who’s help is most necessary to achieve unification.

Gwanhae Market

A 49-year old women working as a supervisor of foreign currency earning company. She graduated from a vocational school. “By establishing the principles of democracy and freedom of movement [with unification] , our country will be able to achieve balanced economic development,” she says.

Suwon Market

A 54-year old man working as a supervisor at the provincial Department of Propaganda and Agitation. He earned a degree from Chongjin University of Mining and Metallurgy. He says that through unification “the economic success of the South will help the North develop its economy, and of course then we'll all live better.”
